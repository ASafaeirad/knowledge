Steroid hormones are a type of hormone made from [[Cholesterol]] that play vital roles in regulating many body processes, including metabolism, immune function, salt and water balance, development of sexual characteristics, and the response to stress.

- They are lipid-soluble, meaning they can easily pass through cell membranes.
- Once inside a cell, they bind to specific receptors (usually inside the cytoplasm or nucleus).
- The hormone–receptor complex then directly influences gene expression, turning certain genes on or off.


| Hormone Type      | Example      | Produced In            | Main Role                   |
| ----------------- | ------------ | ---------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Glucocorticoid    | Cortisol     | Adrenal cortex         | Stress response, metabolism |
| Mineralocorticoid | Aldosterone  | Adrenal cortex         | Water/salt balance          |
| Androgen          | Testosterone | Testes/adrenal glands  | Male traits, muscle         |
| Estrogen          | Estradiol    | Ovaries/adrenal glands | Female traits               |
| Progestogen       | Progesterone | Ovaries/placenta       | Pregnancy maintenance       |
